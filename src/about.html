About Us

What is NAACM?

This market is a gathering of Native American and Indigenous artists and crafters from a diverse
representation of Tribal Nations. The first Sunday Native American arts & crafts market is,
derived from the annual Eugene Winter arts & crafts market which first began in 2013. Held
annually at the Amazon community center, this market began as a thought to bring local Native
American community artists and crafters together to sell their creations to our local Eugene and
Springfield communities during the winter holiday season. That first market was comprised of a
group of Native community friends and family who lived in Eugene and Springfield.

Who We Are

We are a group of local Native artists and crafters who volunteer our time organizing the market
and dedicated to preserving and promoting Native arts and cultures. It is our mission to provide
a safe and supportive venue for the creativity of Native artisans who create authentic Native
American and Indigenous art individually or with their families.

The Eugene Native American Arts & Crafts Market is held on the first Sunday of the month at
the Eugene Farmers Market Pavilion during the months of June thru December 2023.
