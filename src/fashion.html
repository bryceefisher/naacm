Native American Arts & Crafts Market

Fashion Show Call to Artists

November 2024

Overview

Futurism Inspired by Tradition: What Does Indigenous Fashion Mean to You?

The Eugene Native American Arts & Crafts Market (NAACM) invites artists and designers to bring their personal style and apply for the first annual fashion show. Artists and designers are encouraged to respond to the prompt, "Futurism Inspired by Tradition. What does Indigenous Fashion Mean to You?"

The event will take place during the first week of November, 2024. Time and Location to be announced soon.

Background

NAACM will present the first annual fashion show, Futurism Inspired by Tradition: What does Indigenous Fashion Mean to You? This NAACM is a gathering of Native American artists and crafters who live in the Pacific Northwest and are dedicated to preserving Native arts and cultures through arts, craft, and design. The NAACM’s mission is to provide a supportive space for the creativity of Native and Indigenous artists, designers, and crafters in our area who create their own art, crafts, and designs individually or with their families. Artists and crafters come from a diverse representation of Tribal Nations within the United States.

Goals

  • Present distinct fashion, art, craft, and design by Native and Indigenous artists that highlight the intersection of art, fashion, and culture.
  • Raise awareness of the Native and Indigenous arts, artists, designers, and performers in Eugene and beyond.
  • Provide a platform for beginning and advanced Native and Indigenous artists.
  • Promote the Native American Arts and Craft Market and mission.

Eligibility of Applicants

Open to American Indian, Alaskan Native, Native Hawai’ian, and Indigenous artists, designers, and crafters from Canada, Mexico, Central America, South America, Pacific Island, and New Zealand. Youth and adults are welcome to apply. Open to individuals, families, and collectives. Submitting an application to the NAACM fashion show indicates that you are in compliance with the Indian Arts and Crafts Act of 1990 (P.L. 101-644).

Selection Process

A selection committee is formed and led by a NAACM subcommittee and composed of Indigenous and non-Indigenous administrators and/or artists working with NAACM, arts, and/or culture. Artist selections are made through an organized juried process. The Selection Committee is committed to managing a transparent selection process. Our practices and policies help to ensure all applications are considered fairly and outlined below.

  • Decisions are made by consensus, with thought and discussion during deliberations.
  • Identify direct, indirect, and perceived conflicts of interests, and manage them individually.
  • Consider thematic concepts, and represent the diversity of artistic and cultural perspectives from Native and Indigenous artists and designers.
  • Deliberations are confidential, we greatly appreciate feedback on our processes.

Selection consideration includes the following:

  • Description of runway plan with proposed artworks
  • Quality of images
  • Creative concept
  • Original design idea
  • Craftsmanship
  • Body of work including clothing, jewelry and accessories
  • Completed application

Please note, artists and designers are not required to produce every element of the design or look. For example, your concept may be a combination of premade clothing along with regalia, jewelry and/or accessories. Family heirloom or regalia items may also be included.

If ceremonial items are incorporated, the selection committee will review your Description of Presentation Plan and how the items will be used.

NAACM Fashion Show is a community based and family friendly event. The selection committee will take into consideration any use of profanity, nudity, and violence. Hate speech will not be accepted.

Application Submission Requirements

If you have trouble with filling out an online application and need assistance through the process, please contact Chanin Santiago at CSantiago@eugene-or.gov.

Helpful tip! Download the application PDF and write your responses or write on a separate paper. Then type directly into the form when you are ready to apply. Remember to hit SUBMIT.

Please include:

  • Five to eight images of examples of work (work can be existing or in progress) (jpeg, size)
  • Description of Runway Plan (under 500 words)
  • Type of Work- We are currently seeking applications from artists specializing in fashion, textiles, graphic design, jewelry and accessories.
  • Artist Statement (under 250 words) Please describe your work including the medium and materials you use, how you create your designs, your inspiration and any other details you think are important for us to know.
  • Artist biography (under 250 words) optional
